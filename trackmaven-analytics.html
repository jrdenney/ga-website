Tracking Down Trends

The fifth General Assembly project I completed was a first for GA: working with the local marketing analytics company TrackMaven. They wanted us to create tools to help their engineers better understand, predict, and analyze engagement on the social media accounts of some of their top clients.

Once again working with the team from the Chicago West Nile project, we devised methods to measure engagement and give insight on what topics, what hashtags, and what messages drove the most engagement across Facebook and Instagram.

If you're curious to see more of our insights and code set, the PowerPoint slides and GitHub repository for this project are linked below. The repository is controlled by my teammate, Steve Masschelin.

PowerPoint Slides

GitHub Repository

This project was particularly timely in that as the last project before the capstone, it provided a concrete example of a problem that a real-world company might need to solve for one of its clients. The importance of producing quality code and insights were highlighted in this project, and it was a great opportunity for both General Assembly and TrackMaven to collaborate.
